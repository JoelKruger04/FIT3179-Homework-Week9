Major Rail Projects and Rolling Stock

Due to the rapid population growth of Melbourne in the past 50 years, the rail network has to evolve to keep up with demand. Although rolling stock has been evolving, with higher capacity trains to become more common, the last major rail project completed in Melbourne was the City Loop, which opened more than 40 years ago. However, the Metro Tunnel is set to open next year, boosting the capacity of the Pakenham, Cranbourne and Sunbury lines.

ðŸš†The Present: The Utility of Trains in 2024

Despite the rapid increase of Melbourne's population, the usage of trains, trams and buses is lower than it was in 2019. This is mostly due to changes in the lives of many Melburnians, with work and study now commonly undertaken at home, especially when public transport is found to be inconvenient. Nevertheless, investment in the rail network will always be crucial to reducing road congestion and increase access to essential services.

Rethinking the Morning Commute

Despite the rise of remote work, the majority of Australians still drive themselves to work. Although convenient, this reliance on personal vehicles contributes to traffic congestion and urban pollution. The prioritisation of public transport, along with more flexible work and study arrangements, would allow Australians to reduce their environmental impact, as well as decrease travel times for all.

The map shows the average weekday patronage of each metropolitan train station, overlayed in front of the population growth for each LGA (local government area). The high concentation of rail traffic in the inner city is due to the radial nature of Melbourne's train network and the lack of any linkages of train lines.

The concentration of population growth in the CBD (shown in blue) highlights the concentration of Melbourne's economic activity in the inner city. The proposed Suburban Rail Loop (stations in orange) aims to alleviate this flaw by allowing travellers to travel around the city and avoid the CBD, while creating economic hubs that are easily accessible
